The capital of Taiwan is Taipei. Here are some details about it: 1. **Name**: The official name in Chinese is "Taipei" (台北), and it's often romanized as "Táiběi" or "Taibei". In Hokkien, a dialect widely spoken in Taiwan, it's known as "Tâi-pak". 2. **Population**: According to the Taipei City Government, the estimated population of Taipei was 2.68 million as of 2021. 3. **History**: Taipei has been the capital since 1895 when Taiwan was ceded to Japan. After World War II, the Republic of China (now based in Taiwan) took over and made Taipei its capital. 4. **Landmarks**: Some notable landmarks in Taipei include: - Taipei 101: Once the world's tallest building, it's now a symbol of Taiwan. - National Palace Museum: Known for its extensive collection of Chinese imperial artifacts. - Longshan Temple and Bopiliao Historic Block: Two well-preserved examples of old Taiwanese architecture. 5. **Governance**: Taipei is administered as a special municipality, similar to other major cities in Taiwan like Kaohsiung and Taichung.
